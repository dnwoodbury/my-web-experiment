Designing the future of Learning Spaces

Department Head, Learning Spaces & Services at North Carolina State University Libraries. Specializing in makerspaces, digital media studios, and technology-rich environments for higher education.

Biography

Innovating Academic Libraries

I serve as the Department Head for Learning Spaces & Services at the North Carolina State University Libraries. In this role, I manage technology-rich learning environments at both the D.H. Hill Jr. and James B. Hunt Jr. Libraries.

My work focuses on the intersection of physical space and emerging technology. I oversee makerspaces, digital media labs, virtual reality exploration rooms, and collaborative computing areas. My goal is to help lead innovative teaching and learning programs that support students and faculty in the digital age.

Previously, I worked in the Higher Education Division of John Wiley & Sons Publishers. I hold a Master of Science in Information Science from UNC-Chapel Hill and a B.A. in English from the University of Kansas.

Areas of Expertise

Management & Leadership

Leading a department of librarians and specialists, providing instruction and supporting a diverse portfolio of experiential learning environments.

Makerspaces

Managing creative spaces that offer 3D printing, scanning, and prototyping tools for student innovation.

Digital Media

Overseeing audio production studios and digital media creation facilities.

VR & AR

Developing Virtual and Augmented Reality exploration rooms and services.

Learning Spaces

Designing and implementing innovative learning environments that facilitate hands-on, experiential activities.

Open Education

Supporting the adoption and creation of Open Educational Resources (OER).
